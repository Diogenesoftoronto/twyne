# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Writers, editors, researchers, and technically comfortable creators who want a focused drafting room with structured editorial feedback. They are usually trying to move from a vague brief or partial draft into a clearer manuscript, then export or publish it.

## Product Purpose

Twyne is a local-first writing desk with an AI-assisted room of editors. It helps writers define a project dossier, draft in rich text, receive persona feedback, score work against a rubric, manage citations, sync across devices when signed in, and publish or export finished work. Success means the writer keeps ownership and momentum while the product makes revision concrete.

## Brand Personality

Editorial, exacting, literary. The product should feel like a working writer's room rather than generic SaaS: opinionated, calm, and serious about craft without getting precious.

## Anti-references

Do not make Twyne look like a generic AI chat app, a growth dashboard, a blank note-taking clone, or a loud marketing funnel. Avoid decorative UI that competes with the manuscript, vague AI productivity claims, and controls that obscure where drafts and keys are stored.

## Design Principles

- Keep the manuscript central.
- Make data movement explicit: local, synced, published, or sent to a provider.
- Use standard controls for standard tasks, dressed in Twyne's editorial system.
- Prefer concise copy that names the actual action.
- Preserve writer trust through clear export, privacy, and account controls.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Aim for WCAG AA contrast and keyboard-operable controls. Important interactions should work with semantic HTML where possible. Respect reduced-motion preferences, avoid color-only state, and keep long-form reading comfortable on mobile and desktop.
